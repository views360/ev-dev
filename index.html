How the calculator works

Break-Even Mode

Two input values are used to calculate the raw break-even-miles value for each provider subscription. It is a blunt instrument because it offers no information about overall journey costs and timings. These are available in Cost-Reducation Mode, which requires far more information for its calculations.

  1. Vehicle Efficiency (Miles per kilowatt-hour): This value determines your range on a single charge. Since EV efficiency fluctuates with seasonal temperatures, enter an up-to-date value for the most accurate calculation.
  2. Typical Average PAYG Public Rate You Pay (pence per kilowatt-hour): This value is the average PAYG rate you expect to pay at public chargers. It serves as the baseline for comparing costs against provider subscription plans.

Cost-Reduction Mode

10 different input values are used in a complex series of calculations to provide not only the lowest cost for your journey(s) but also provide a comparison between the cheapest subscription provider and PAYG rates, the amount of overall charging time needed for the specified journey length(s), and even a real-world itinerary detailing the charging points and durations. It ain't rocket science, but it's the next best thing.

  1. Journey Distance (miles): This is your total journey distance, from start to finish (e.g., from leaving home to arriving back), rather than individual legs of a trip.
  2. Usable Battery Size (kilowatt-hours): This value calculates your distance between charges, based on your recharge threshold and departing battery level.
  3. Pre-Charge Battery Level (battery %): Your battery level before charging for the first journey. The difference between this and your Departure Battery Level determines the initial pre-charge cost.
  4. Departure Battery Level (battery %): This is your target battery level for the first charge. It determines the initial pre-journey cost. While 80% is standard to maintain battery health, charging to 100% is acceptable for long trips if you depart soon after.
  5. Minimum Desired Charging Speed (kilowatts): This is your minimum acceptable public charging speed. Since some providers offer lower rates for slower chargers, setting this threshold prevents slow speeds from skewing your results if you prefer faster turnarounds.
  6. Vehicle Efficiency (Miles per kilowatt-hour): This value determines your range on a single charge. Since EV efficiency fluctuates with seasonal temperatures, enter an up-to-date value for the most accurate calculation.
  7. Pre-Charge Rate (pence per kilowatt-hour): The cost to charge your battery before departing (e.g., your cheap overnight tariff). It is used to calculate the pre-charge cost and the overall journey total.
  8. Max. Charging Speed (kilowatts): This is your battery's maximum charging speed, which is used to calculate realistic charging durations.
  9. Recharge Threshold (battery %): The minimum battery level you allow before recharging. It determines the number of charging stops you will required. While this level varies per stop, it averages out over your journey (%). It defaults to 20%, the recommended level for long-term battery health.
  10. Typical Average PAYG Public Rate You Pay (pence per kilowatt-hour): This value is the average PAYG rate you expect to pay at public chargers. It serves as the baseline for comparing costs against provider subscription plans.
